PROJECT 02 / 11 · Senior Project · Initial Design

3D-Printed Flying Wing

The initial design for our senior project — a 3D-printed RC flying wing optimized for high glide ratio, taken from an airfoil study through material testing and structural FEA before the team iterated to an updated competition design.

TypeRC flying wing (initial design)
ObjectiveMaximize glide ratio
AeroXFLR5 · 5 airfoils
Material3D-printed PLA (FDM)
Mat. testASTM D638 tensile
StructureANSYS Static Structural
Overview

The goal was a printable flying wing that glides efficiently. I used XFLR5 to compare five airfoils — MH60, MH45, SD7037, Clark Y, and E325 — evaluating lift-to-drag and pitching behavior to choose a section that balances glide performance with the stability a tailless wing demands.

Because the airframe is 3D printed in PLA, handbook material values don't apply. I ran ASTM D638 tensile tests on printed coupons to measure real modulus and strength, then fed those into ANSYS Static Structural FEA on the wing spars to confirm they survive flight and wing-loading conditions.

I validated the structure physically with a sandbag wing-loading test and a vibration test setup, and printed the wing in segments of varying density to manage mass distribution along the span.

Highlights
  • Five-airfoil trade study in XFLR5
  • PLA characterized experimentally via ASTM D638
  • Spar FEA in ANSYS using measured material properties
  • Sandbag wing-loading and vibration validation
