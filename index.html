About Me

ABOUT
ME

I'm a Mechanical Engineering student at Cal Poly SLO, graduating December 2026 and specializing in composite structures and manufacturing. I work at the intersection of hands-on fabrication and structural analysis.

During the 2024–2025 season with Cal Poly Racing's Baja SAE team, I manufactured composite components including a Carbon-Kevlar skid plate, steering wheel inserts, and safety hardware — helping the team achieve 6th overall at Baja SAE Arizona 2025 and pass technical inspection first with no safety concerns.

My senior capstone is a 3D-printed competition flying wing aircraft designed for high glide ratio. I'm also actively pursuing internship opportunities in aerospace, automotive, and advanced manufacturing.

Composite Skid Plate
Fabricated an impact-resistant three-piece vehicle skid plate for off-road racing. Carbon-Kevlar with Rohacell core via vacuum infusion — 43% lighter than aluminum. Achieved 6th overall at Baja SAE Arizona 2025 with no structural damage.

SAMPE Composite I-Beam
Designed and manufactured the lightest composite I-beam for maximum point load in three-point bending. Unidirectional pre-preg carbon fiber with Nomex sandwich core. Achieved 9kN load at just 312g.
Pre-preg Carbon Nomex Core Three-Point Bend SAMPE

03
CF Miata Radiator Cowl
Designing and fabricating a lightweight carbon fiber cowl cover to direct increased airflow through the radiator of an NA Mazda Miata. Pre-preg plate cured to final dimensions with water jet cutouts. Currently in progress.

04
Steering Wheel Inserts
Manufactured six aluminum inserts for a composite steering wheel using a manual lathe. Parts turned and faced to within 0.005" of drawing dimensions. Used in the 2025 Baja SAE competition with no damage to the composite structure.

ASTM D3039/D3518 Testing
Fabricated and tested unidirectional composite samples using Toray TC250 infused pre-preg. Tested in Instron — experimental results matched analytical micromechanics predictions within 2% error.
